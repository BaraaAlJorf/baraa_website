About Me

I’m a PhD researcher at NYU in Computer Science, working on AI systems for healthcare — with a focus on clinical foundation models, multimodal learning, and medical data. My work integrates clinical notes, medical imaging, and EHRs to build machine learning systems that can support diagnosis, risk prediction, and clinical decision-making. Recently, I completed a Visiting PhD semester at Columbia University, where I took advanced coursework in causal inference and foundation model theoretical design. I’ve published research on fusion architectures and clinical benchmarks, presented at international AI and healthcare conferences, and helped lead events like the Clinical AI Bootcamp and Healthcare AI Summit. Open to research collaborations, clinical-AI roles, and opportunities that connect machine learning with real-world healthcare impact.
